Description:
Abstract:
The Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) Sectional Raster Aeronautical Chart series is designed to meet the needs of users who require georeferenced raster images of FAA Visual Flight Rules (VFR) Sectional Aeronautical Charts. A Sectional Raster Aeronautical Chart is a raster image of an FAA Sectional Aeronautical Chart. The image inside the neat line is georeferenced to the surface of the earth. Only the main body of the chart is accurately georeferenced. Georeferencing information for insets except for the Hawaiian Islands should be disregarded. The raster data may be viewed using a geographical information system to take advantage of the georeferenced nature of the data or the data can be viewed using commonly available image viewing software.
Purpose:
The Sectional Raster Aeronautical Chart series has been developed to meet the needs of many users. The data has the flexibility of being useful for both industry and individuals. Additional digital data may easily be overlaid on the raster image. Data such as weather, temporary flight restrictions, obstacles, or other geospatial data can be combined with Sectional Raster Aeronautical Chart data to support a variety of needs.
Supplemental_Information:
Each Sectional Raster Aeronautical Chart is provided as one file. Western Aleutian Islands is divided into an East side and a West side. Hawaiian Islands includes Honolulu, Mariana Islands and Samoan Islands Insets as separate georeferenced files. Each file name is structured by chart name and edition number, e.g., St Louis 67.tif. The files are 300 dots per inch and 8-bit color. The data is provided as a GeoTIFF.
